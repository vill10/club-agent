# STYLE — writing contract

How we write copy and docs in this repo. Two audiences, two registers.

- **User-facing copy** (UI strings, chat replies, empty states, errors): plain,
  direct, natural Russian. Write like a helpful person texting a parent — short
  sentences, no marketing gloss, no jargon. Say what the thing does.
- **Docs** (README, DESIGN, code comments, this file): concise English. Lead with
  the point. Cut throat-clearing.

## Banned words and structures

These read as AI-slop. Do not use them, in either language, anywhere a human reads:

- "seamless", "robust", "load-bearing", "elevate", "empower", "delve",
  "tapestry", "unleash"
- "leverage" as a verb (use "use")
- "in today's fast-paced world" and any cousin opener
- em-dash overuse — one per paragraph at most; prefer a period or comma
- rule-of-three filler in marketing copy: the "X, Y, and Z" triad padded for
  rhythm rather than meaning
- the negation-pivot: "It's not just X, it's Y." Just say Y.

The Russian equivalents are banned too: no «бесшовный», «раскрыть потенциал»,
«в современном быстро меняющемся мире», «погрузиться», and so on. If a phrase
exists only to sound impressive, cut it.

## Positive rules

1. One idea per sentence. If you need a comma to bolt on a second clause, ask
   whether it should be a second sentence.
2. Concrete over abstract. "Находит кружки рядом с домом" beats "оптимизирует
   поиск активностей".
3. Active voice. Name who does what.
4. No exclamation marks in UI copy unless a real person would use one.
5. Numbers as numerals (3 клуба, not три клуба) in UI.
6. When unsure, read it aloud. If you wouldn't say it to a parent across a table,
   rewrite it.
